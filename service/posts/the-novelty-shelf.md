A dedicated shelf for the pieces that don't fit anywhere else — the experiments, the weird commissions, the things that made sense at the time and still kind of do.

The novelty shelf started as a joke. I had a few pieces that were too strange for the main shop display but too good (or at least too interesting) to put away. So they got their own shelf. Now it's become a thing.

## what's on it

Right now: three bongs in varying stages of finishing, a mug shaped like it's melting that I threw as a technical experiment, two small sculptures that came out of a week where I was just messing around, a booby vase that a friend commissioned as a gift and that I ended up making four of because they're genuinely fun to throw, and a small angry-looking face jug that I have no explanation for.

## on making weird things

The novelty pieces are where I experiment most freely. There's less pressure when you're not trying to make something that has to function as dinnerware.

A lot of what I've learned about form and proportion has come from making things that push against the expected shapes. When you try to make something deliberately strange, you learn a lot about why conventional forms work the way they do.

## commissions for weird stuff

I take commissions for novelty pieces occasionally. If you have a specific concept, get in touch — I'm generally up for it if it's technically feasible. The stranger the better, honestly.
