A look at the corner of the studio where most of the work actually happens — the wheel, the shelves of drying work, and approximately seventeen containers of slip that definitely all need to exist.

The studio is in the back half of the garage. It's not glamorous — exposed ceiling, concrete floor, one small window — but it works. The wheel is centred in the space and everything else radiates out from there.

## the shelving situation

I built the drying shelves out of basic timber and they've been upgraded several times as the volume of work increased. Right now there are three levels running along the back wall, plus a rolling cart that goes in and out depending on what's firing.

Everything on the shelves is either drying, waiting to be trimmed, or waiting to be bisque fired. There's almost always something at every stage simultaneously.

## tools

The tool collection has grown way beyond what I actually need. Somewhere along the way I accumulated: four different loop tools, two sets of ribs (metal and rubber), more wire tools than any one person requires, a needle tool I can never find, and a wooden knife I made in the first week that I still use constantly.

The useful tools live in a jar on the wheel tray. The rest live in a drawer that I rarely open.

## what's on the shelf right now

Currently drying: a batch of mugs from earlier this week, three large bowls that need trimming tomorrow, and a few experimental forms I'm not sure about yet. The kiln is sitting empty waiting for the next bisque load.
