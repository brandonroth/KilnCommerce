Every kiln opening is a gamble. You load it hoping for the best and pull out a mix of triumphs, disasters, and a few pieces that make you question everything you thought you knew about your own glazes.

This last firing was a good one overall. Lost two pieces to cracking — one mug that had a hidden S-crack in the base (my fault, rushed the drying) and a small bowl that just decided it didn't want to exist anymore. Everything else made it through.

## the good

The set of four mugs I threw for a custom order came out exactly right. The celadon pooled beautifully in the grooves I carved around the bottom third. I was nervous about those — the customer wanted a specific colour match — but the results were better than the sample.

Also pulled out a couple of bongs that have the best surface texture I've gotten so far. Something about this firing temperature was perfect for the matte black glaze.

## the not so good

I tried a new ash glaze recipe on three test tiles and a small vase. The tiles were interesting — unpredictable in a good way. The vase, though, had massive crawling across the shoulder. The glaze just beaded up and pulled away. Back to the drawing board on that one.

## next firing

Loading up again next week. I've got a backlog of pieces that have been waiting — mostly shop stock and a few experiments I want to get through.
